General background

The School of Agriculture and Agricultural Technology was established on 17th February 2021 by the proclamation of the Academic Board at its 377th Statutory meeting. Presently the school offers accredited Programmes of both National Diploma and Higher National Diploma

The National Diploma Programme are in three Department viz

  • Agricultural Technology Department
  • Fisheries Technology Department
  • Horticultural Technology Department

At the Higher National Diploma level. The Agricultural Technology Department offers the following:

  • Higher National Diploma in Animal Production
  • Higher National Diploma in Extension Management
  • Higher National Diploma in Crop Production

Entry Requirements for National Diploma

The minimium entry requirements into the National Diploma Programme in school of Agriculture and Agricultural Technology is five credits level passes in the West African School Certificates, Senior Secondary School Certificates, General Certificate of Education (GCE) Ordinary level Obtained at not more than two sittings is also allowed
Five Credit level passes in National Examination Council (NECO) at not more than two sittings is also accepted.
The same goes for National Business and Technical Education Board (NABTEB). The Subjects must include Mathematics, Biology/Agriicultural Science/Animal Husbandary/Fisheries, Chemistry and any one of the following Geography, Economics, Statictics, Physics and at least a Credit pass in English Language is compulsory.

Entry Requirements for Higher National Diploma

Candidates for Admission into Higher National Diploma in Agricultural Technology at Lower Credit above the Federal Polytechnic Ado-Ekiti or any other recognized higher institution of equivalent standard plus ONE YEAR post ND industrial attachment.
IN addititon, National Diploma holder must possess five credits in WASSCE, GCE, NABTEB, SSCE, or NECO at not more than two sittings.
The five credits must include Mathematics, Biology/Agricultural sciences/Animal Husbandary/Fisheries, Chemistry and any of the following, Geography, Economics, Statistics, Physics, and at least a credit pass in English Language is compulsory. (A Candidates with a pass grade at the ND level after a minimum of two years industrial attachment may be considered)

Curriculum Structure

The national Diploma in Agricultural Technology Programme is structured to last for two sessions (Four semesters). This incorporates three to four months of supervised Industrial Work Experience Scheme.
The Higher National Diploma in Agricultural Technology Programme with options in Agricultural Extensions & Management, and animal Production are also structured to last for two sessions (four semesters).
