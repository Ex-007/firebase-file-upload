General background

The School of Engineering comprises Agricultural and Bio-Enviromental Engineering, Civil Engineering, Electrical and Elecctronic Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Mineral and Petroleum Resources Engineering.
The School has about three thousand students studying at both the National Diploma and Higher National Diploma levels. The School prepares Diplomates for employment in the Engineering sector. The School graduates can be found across the world putting their practical and academic skills to good use. All the engineering programmes are accredited by the professional bodies and the National Board for Technical Education.
The aim and Objectives of the School remain unchanged, while the entry requirements in the Departments are stated below.

Agricultural and Bio-Enviromental Engineering

The minimum entry requirements for the National Diploma Programme in the Department are: five (5) O-Level in SSCE, GCE, NECO, NCC, NTC or NABTEB, credits passes in relevant subjects including English Language, Physics, Mathematics, Chemistry plus any one of Biology/Agricultural | Animal Husbandary, Technical Drawing, Economics, Further-mathematics, ICT, Data processing, Computer and Statictics.

DEPARTMENT OF CIVIL ENGINEERING

Objectives

The National Diploma Programme in Civil Engineering Technology is aimed at Producing Technicians for both the public and private sectors of the economy who can also be self-employed and self-reliant. In addition, Higher National Diplomates Should be able to play prominent roles in the design, execution and supervision of Civil Engineering works.

Admission Requirements

NATIONAL DIPLOMA

  1. Credit passes in Mathematiccs, Physics, Chemistry, English Language and any one from Geography/Economics, Further Mathematics, Building Consrtruction/Woodwork/Civil Education/Technical Drawing/Metal works and Biology at not more than two sittings in WASSCE or NECO.
  2. Credit passes in Physics, Chemistry, Mathematiccs, English and a trade-related subject such as Building construction, Building Technology, Concrete Technology Woodwork, Technical Drawing at not more than sittings in NABTEB or National Technical Certificate. NB: Trade course must be relevant, e.g. Woodwork, Building Consstruction, Technical Drawing, Metalwork etc.

HIGHER NATIONAL DIPLOMA

  1. To be considered for admission into Civil Engineering Department, a prospective student shall possesses a minimum of lower credit in Civil Engineering from the Federal Polytechnic Ado-Ekiti, while a prospective student with a minimum of upper credit from othe polytechnic may be considered for admission.
  2. A letter of completion of compulsory one-year industrial training duly signed and stamped by the establishment/firm/organization.
  3. The candidate must satisfy admission requirement for National Diploma as spelt out above.

CURRICULUM STRUCTURE

The National Diploma Programme is structured to consist four semesters of classroom, laboratory, workshop, fieldwork and four months of Supervised Industrial Work Experience Scheme. The Higher National Diploma is a terminal Programme Structured to last for two academic sessions (four semesters). Core courses and Specialized (Elective) courses are available for the students to offer for their period of academic programmes.
