General background

The School of Engineering comprises Agricultural and Bio-Enviromental Engineering, Civil Engineering, Electrical and Elecctronic Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Mineral and Petroleum Resources Engineering.
The School has about three thousand students studying at both the National Diploma and Higher National Diploma levels. The School prepares Diplomates for employment in the Engineering sector. The School graduates can be found across the world putting their practical and academic skills to good use. All the engineering programmes are accredited by the professional bodies and the National Board for Technical Education.
The aim and Objectives of the School remain unchanged, while the entry requirements in the Departments are stated below.

ENTRY REQUIREMENTS

Agricultural and Bio-Enviromental Engineering

National Diploma

The minimum entry requirements for the National Diploma Programme in the Department are: five (5) O-Level in SSCE, GCE, NECO, NCC, NTC or NABTEB, credits passes in relevant subjects including English Language, Physics, Mathematics, Chemistry plus any one of Biology/Agricultural | Animal Husbandary, Technical Drawing, Economics, Further-mathematics, ICT, Data processing, Computer and Statictics.

Higher National Diploma

The entry requirements into Higher National Diploma Programme in the Department are:

  1. the minimum entry requirements for the National Diploma
  2. lower credit pass at National Diploma
  3. One year mandatory industrial attachment

DEPARTMENT OF ELECTRICAL ELECTRONIC ENGINEERING

Objectives

The programme is designed to produce Electrical and Electronic Engineering Technicians/technologists for the manufacturing, assembling, servicing and power-generating, transmitting, distributing and utilizing industries.

ENTRY REQUIREMENTS

NATIONAL DIPLOMA

  1. Five Credit level passes in WASSCE, SSCE, NECO OR GCE O'level obtained at not more than two sittings in English, Mathematics, Physics and Chemistry and any other one form Technical Drawing, Further Mathematics, Economics, Geography, Electrical Installation, Computer Studies, Electrical Works, Appliances Maintenance and Repairs and Basic Electronics/Electricity.
  2. NTC - National Technical Certificate issued by NABTEB only in relevant trade, and not in trade areas - certificates in any area such as: Book-keeping, Commerce, Secretarial Studies, Accounting, Data Processing and ICT, etc. is not acceptable in Electrical/Electronic Engineering.

HIGHER NATIONAL DIPLOMA

National Diploma is the pre-requisite, with minimum of Lower Credit and one year Industrial Experience

Curriculum Structure

The National Diploma (ND) programme covers four semesters of classroom, laboratory and workstop activities plus 3-4 months of Supervised Industrial Work Experience Scheme (SIWES). Higher National Diploma (HND) students are expected to offer some common (core) courses as well as specialized courses in any of the following two options:
Electronic and Telecommunication option;
Power and Machines option.
