3.1

General background

The School of Business took off with the Department of Accountancy and Secretarial Studies with less than 100 students. the number of Departments has now increased to nine (9), as listed below:

  • Accountancy
  • Banking and Finance
  • Business Administration and Management
  • General Studies
  • Marketing
  • Mass Communication
  • Office Technology and Management
  • Purchasing and Supply
  • Taxation

The Department admits and graduate students in their respective disciplines, with the exception of the General Studies Department, which services all Departments in the institution. Each Departments offer programmes leading to the award of National Diploma and Higher National Diploma.

3.2

Objectives

The National Diploma programmes is aimed at producing diplomates with sound theoretical and practical skills to carry out a number of activities in the business world effectively.
At the Higher National Diploma level, the programme is designed to produce middle/high level Business Management personnel for both piublic and private business enterprises.

At the end of the programme, the diplomates should be able to function:

  • as middle-level managers and captains of industries;
  • in government organizations and parastatals in order to enhance the economic growth of the country;
  • as private entrepreneurs, setting up their own business and managing them effectively;
  • as top Management personnel in small and medium-sized organizations.

3.3

Entry Requirements for National Diploma

The minimum entry requirement into the National Diploma (ND) programme in the school of Business Studies is five credit passes in the Senior School certificate examination (SSCE) or National Examination Council (Neco) at not more than two sittings. The subjects must include English Language(Literature-in-English) and oral English are not accepted in place of English Language), Mathematics and three other subjects from Economics,Business Methods, principles of Accounts, Literature-in-English, commerce, History, Statistics, Geography, government, Agric. Science/Biology.

However, a candidate may be admitted into the office Technology and Management Department if he has:

  1. Grade II Teachers certificate with five(5) credits including English Language and Mathematics at not more than two sittings, or
  2. an RSA/WEAC Business studies certificate stage II in shorthand, Typewriting, English Language, Mathematics and one other subjects at not more than two sittings, or
  3. five credity passes in the relevant subjects in the National Business and Technical Examination Board (NABTEB)

3.4

Entry Requirements for Higher National Diploma

The minimium entry requirements into the HND programme in the School of Business Studies include:

  1. all the requirements into the ND programme in Business Studies,
  2. a minimium of Lower Credit Pass (CGPA of 2.50) in ND Business Studies or related programme,
  3. a minimum of one year post-ND cognatework experience in internship, and
  4. in exceptional cases, ND diplomates with a Pass (CGPA of 2.00 - 2.49) in the ND examination, with two or more years of work experience in the specific field may be considered for admission into the HND programme.

DEPARTMENT OF MARKETING

3.5.1

Objectives

The Department of Marketing is one of the Department in the School of Business Studies. The Department offers National Diploma and Higher National Diploma in Marketing.
The National Diploma Programme in Marketing is aimed at producing diplomates with appropriate theoretical and practical knowledge to efficiently carry out middle level marketing activities in industry Public Service and as entrepreneurs.
The Higher National Diploma is designed to produce diplomates for the managerial position with thorough theoretical and practical knowledge of marketing operations that will enable them proffer solutions to the marketing problems in organizations (profit and non-profit organizations).
